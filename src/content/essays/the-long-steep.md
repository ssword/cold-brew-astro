Some thoughts are ready the moment they arrive. You can write them down in a
sentence and they hold. Most are not like that. Most need to sit, in the dark,
in cold water, while the parts of them that are merely clever dissolve and the
parts that are true settle to the bottom.

This piece is one of the latter, and I am leaving it steeping on purpose. I have
a claim I half believe: that the uncertainty a language model reports about its
own answer is not noise to be calibrated away but a signal about the shape of
what it was trained on. Where the data was thin, the model hedges. Where the
data was thick and contradictory, it hedges differently. The hedge has a
texture, and the texture is a map.

I cannot defend that yet. When I try to make it precise it slips, and when I
make it vague enough to be safe it stops being interesting. So I am doing the
patient thing instead of the fast thing. I am writing the claim down where you
can see it, marking it unfinished, and coming back to stir it when I have read
more and thought more and been wrong a few more times.

If you are reading this and it still says steeping, that is the point. Watch it
change. The version you serve to guests is not the version you make at midnight,
and neither is finished the way a tweet is finished — closed, performed, gone.
This stays open. Let it.
