There is a moment, early in training, when the loss is still high and the
outputs are still noise, but something has shifted. The gradients have found
a direction. The model has not learned anything you could name yet, but it has
started to lean.

I think about that moment a lot. Not the finished model, not the benchmark, but
the first faint signal that the optimization is going somewhere. It is easy to
miss because nothing looks different. The numbers are bad. The samples are
garbage. And yet the slope is real.

Patience is the only ingredient. You cannot rush the early epochs any more than
you can rush cold brew; steeping is the work, and the work is mostly waiting
while something invisible accumulates.

So this is a blog about the waiting — about the parts of learning, machine and
otherwise, that happen before there is anything to show.
